Applied Engineering Geology Laboratory

Part A

Unit-1 :

Identification of Minerals based on their Physical Properties, Chemical composition and uses.Quartz and its varieties : Rock crystal, Rose quartz, Milky quartz, Amethyst, Grey quartz, Blood stone, Flint, Agate, Chert, Jasper, Chalcedony and Opal.

Unit-2 :

Feldspar group - Orthoclase, Microcline, Plagioclase. Muscovite, Biotite, Hornblende, Augite, Olivine, Serpentine, Asbestos, Kaolin, Talc, Garnet, Corundum, Gypsum and Baryte

Unit-3 :

Carbonates – Calcite, Dolomite, Magnesite. Ore-minerals – Magnetite, Hematite, Limonite, Chromite, Ironpyrite, Chalcopyrite, Pyrolusite, Psilomelane, Bauxite and Galena. Identification of rocks based on their Geological properties.

Unit-4

Igneous rocks : Granite, Syenite, Diorite, Gabbro, Dunite, Porphyres, Dolerite, Pegmatite, Basalt, Rhyolite, and Pumice.

Unit-5

Sedimentary rocks : Sandstone, Limestone, Shale, Breccia, Conglomerate and Laterite.

Unit-6

Metamorphic Rocks : Gneiss, Quartzite, Marble, Slate, Phyllite, Schists and Charnockite.

Unit-7

Thickness problems - 3 Types

Unit-8

Dip and strike problems – 3 Types

Unit-9

Bore hole problems (On level ground)
